# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Pokémon enthusiasts, casual fans, gamers, and researchers who want to browse, search, and explore detailed Pokémon information. Users range from children discovering Pokémon for the first time to adult fans looking up stats, moves, and type matchups. Context: casual browsing, often on mobile or desktop, in relaxed/recreational settings.

## Product Purpose

A multi-page Pokédex application built on the PokeAPI. It lets users browse all Pokémon with infinite scroll, search with debounce, filter by type, and sort by ID or name. Each Pokémon has a dedicated detail page showing stats, abilities, moves, sprites, and species information. Success means finding any Pokémon quickly and understanding its characteristics at a glance.

## Brand Personality

Playful, nostalgic, colorful, friendly, enthusiastic. The interface should feel like a modern take on the classic Pokédex device: approachable, information-dense but not overwhelming, and genuinely fun to use.

## Anti-references

- Corporate SaaS dashboards (navy, sterile, metric-heavy)
- Generic card-grid templates (icon + title + description repeated endlessly)
- Dark-mode-everything crypto/NFT aesthetic
- Overly minimalist designs that strip away personality
- Bootstrap-looking default components

## Design Principles

1. **Celebrate color**: Pokémon's identity is its type colors. Use them purposefully, not generically.
2. **Exploration is delight**: Browsing should feel like discovery, not database querying.
3. **Data density with hierarchy**: Detail pages are information-rich. Group and prioritize so the eye knows where to land first.
4. **Snappy and responsive**: Search, filter, and scroll should feel immediate.
5. **Nostalgia without kitsch**: Reference the classic Pokédex feel without becoming a toy.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA target
- Type color indicators must not rely on color alone (use icons/patterns)
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`
- Ensure sufficient contrast for text over type-colored backgrounds
